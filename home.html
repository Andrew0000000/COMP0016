Abstract

The NHS serves (millions?) of people around the UK every day. With the increasing number of people suffering from various health problems and the outbreak of COVID-19 in the last several years, the supply of services are simply struggling to keep up with the demand. This has led to unreasonably long wait times for some patients requiring urgent medical attention, which may put their lives in danger. It also means that NHS staff are facing immense stress in their everyday work.

In order to reduce the workload of NHS staff and shorten the waiting time for patients, we believe implementing automation could be a viable solution. IBM Watson provides natural language processing capabilities, which can be used to automatically schedule patient appointments by reading text messages. Microsoft Power Platform allows users to interact with APIs without having to code hands-on through custom connectors.

Our idea has been tested by some NHS workers and based on the feedback received, it has successfully reduced their workload as a lot of repetitive tasks can now be completed using automation.
